#### Plan Differences

Comet replaces five Scan parquet operators with CometNativeScan for tables: store_sales (via store), customer, customer_address, date_dim, and household_demographics. Six ColumnarToRow operators are replaced with CometNativeColumnarToRow. Five Filter operators become CometFilter and four Project operators become CometProject. Four Sort operators are replaced by three CometSort operators. Four of five Exchange (shuffle) operators are replaced: three become CometExchange and one becomes CometColumnarExchange. One of four BroadcastExchange operators becomes CometBroadcastExchange, and one of four BroadcastHashJoin operators becomes CometBroadcastHashJoin. Nine WholeStageCodegen nodes (5, 7-14) are eliminated. AQEShuffleRead count drops from 2 to 1. Total node count drops from 68 to 59 (-9 nodes).

The structural change that matters most is the replacement of one BroadcastExchange + AQEShuffleRead with CometBroadcastExchange + CometColumnarExchange. In the Default plan, AQE coalesces 200 partitions down to 9 for the broadcast collection stage. In Comet, the CometColumnarExchange retains 200 partitions.

#### Root Cause

The 471ms regression is driven by two factors that outweigh Comet's processing speed improvements:

1. **Scan-phase GC overhead (+327ms on critical path):** The store_sales scan stage (7210) takes 2.955s in Comet vs 2.606s in Default (9908), despite scanning identical data (4.7 GB, 324M records). CometNativeScan produces columnar batches that are then converted via CometNativeColumnarToRow. GC time in this stage is 1553ms in Comet vs 388ms in Default (+1165ms aggregate across tasks). Per-task p50 duration is 165ms longer in Comet (1.916s vs 1.751s). Since this is the longest scan stage and all subsequent work depends on it, the 349ms stage-duration difference translates directly to critical-path delay.

2. **Broadcast collection stage expansion (+535ms stage time, +541ms on critical path):** Default's broadcast collection (stage 9915) uses an AQEShuffleRead that coalesces 200 partitions into 9 tasks, completing in 33ms. Comet replaces this with CometColumnarExchange retaining the full 200 partitions (stage 7217), requiring 200 tasks and 568ms. The per-task work is comparable (p50 27ms vs 24ms), but scheduling and executing 200 tasks vs 9 creates substantial overhead.

3. **Inter-job scheduling gap (+591ms):** After the broadcast collection job completes, there is a 667ms gap before the next job starts in Comet, compared to only 76ms in Default. This gap occurs between job 3407 (completion: 08.941) and job 3408 (submission: 09.608). The broadcast result from the CometBroadcastExchange with 200 partitions likely takes longer to materialize on the driver before the next job can be planned and submitted.

These three factors add approximately +1.46s to the critical path. They are partially offset by Comet's faster processing in the join/aggregate stages: stage 7213 is 461ms faster than 9911, and stage 7219 is 532ms faster than 9917 (due to Comet's reduced shuffle read of 211.5 MB total vs 1.3 GB, enabled by columnar shuffle that produces more compact data and avoids one shuffle materialization entirely). The net effect is the observed +471ms regression.

The aggregate stage time is actually 1.938s lower in Comet (8.106s vs 10.044s), and total tasks are lower (1115 vs 1849), confirming that Comet does less total work. The regression is purely a critical-path scheduling effect: the broadcast collection stage's inability to leverage AQE partition coalescing, combined with increased GC pressure in the scan stage, serializes enough additional latency to negate Comet's per-stage processing gains.
