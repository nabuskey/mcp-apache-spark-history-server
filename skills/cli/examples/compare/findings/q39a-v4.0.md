#### Plan Differences

Comet replaced a subset of operators in the query plan with native equivalents:

- **Scans**: `Scan parquet` on `date_dim` (2), `item` (1), `warehouse` (1) replaced with `CometNativeScan` equivalents. The `inventory` scans (the large 25-task and 20-task scans) remained as Spark `Scan parquet`.
- **Exchanges**: 3 of 5 `Exchange` nodes replaced -- 1 `CometExchange`, 2 `CometColumnarExchange`, 1 `CometBroadcastExchange` (replacing 1 `BroadcastExchange`). The remaining 2 `Exchange` nodes stayed as Spark.
- **Joins**: 1 of 7 `BroadcastHashJoin` replaced with `CometBroadcastHashJoin`. The remaining 6 stayed as Spark.
- **Filters/Projects**: 4 `Filter` replaced with `CometFilter`, 2 `Project` replaced with `CometProject`.
- **Sort**: 1 `Sort` replaced with `CometSort`.
- **ColumnarToRow**: 4 `ColumnarToRow` removed, replaced with 5 `CometNativeColumnarToRow`.
- **WholeStageCodegen**: 6 WholeStageCodegen nodes removed (7-12), 1 added (4). This reflects the portion of the plan that runs natively in Comet.

The plan has 72 nodes in Default vs 67 in Comet (-5), but 66 edges in Comet vs 65 in Default (+1), indicating a slight structural difference in the DAG topology.

#### Root Cause

The regression has two contributing factors:

1. **Slower scan stages on the critical path (+330-538ms wall clock)**: The inventory table scans (stages 1429/6552 and 1430/6553) are the bottleneck in both apps due to a single highly-skewed task. In Comet, the median task time is 35-77% higher (234ms vs 173ms for the 25-task scan; 230ms vs 130ms for the 20-task scan). These scan stages use standard Spark `Scan parquet` (not CometNativeScan), but the downstream Comet operators require data in Arrow columnar format. The `ColumnarToRow` conversions in Default are replaced by `CometNativeColumnarToRow` in Comet, and the presence of `CometColumnarExchange` as the shuffle writer means the scan tasks must serialize data into the Comet columnar shuffle format rather than Spark's row-based `UnsafeRow` format. This increases per-task compute time at the scan stage.

2. **Partition count inflation in the broadcast exchange path (+326ms)**: Default's plan uses a `hashpartitioning(w_warehouse_sk, inv_item_sk, 7)` exchange before the broadcast, collecting the aggregated data into 7 partitions. Comet replaces this with a `CometColumnarExchange` that retains 200 partitions (matching `spark.sql.shuffle.partitions`). This results in 200 tasks (vs 7) in the broadcast stage, 3.2x more shuffle data (23.3 MB vs 7.2 MB due to Arrow columnar encoding overhead across 200 partitions), and 11.2x longer stage duration (358ms vs 32ms). The downstream post-join stages also inherit wider partitioning, running 25 tasks instead of 9 and writing 5.4x more shuffle data. The final stage runs 3 tasks instead of 1, adding 95ms.

The aggregate effect is approximately +1.2s wall-clock regression. The scan stage slowdown accounts for roughly half, and the broadcast exchange partition inflation accounts for the other half. GC time is lower in Comet (-323ms), partially offsetting the overhead. The total task count increase from 483 to 710 (+47%) is almost entirely attributable to the broadcast exchange path: the 200-task broadcast stage alone adds 193 tasks over Default's 7-task stage, and the wider post-join stages add the remaining ~34 tasks.
